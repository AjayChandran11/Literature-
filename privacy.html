Privacy Policy

Literature — The Classic Card Game

Last updated: March 20, 2026
Literature does not require account registration and does not sell or share your personal data with third parties.

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Literature ("the app", "we") handles information when you use the app on your Android device. By using Literature, you agree to the practices described below. We process data based on legitimate interest to ensure app stability and functionality.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the minimum information necessary to provide the app's features:

  • Player name — the name you enter before starting a game. This is used solely to identify you to other players during an online session and is never stored on our servers beyond the duration of the game session.
  • Gameplay data — moves made during a game (card asks, claims) are transmitted to our server only in online multiplayer mode to keep all players in sync. This data is held in memory for the duration of the session and discarded when the session ends.
  • Crash reports — if the app crashes, anonymised diagnostic information (stack trace, device model, Android version, app version) is automatically sent to Firebase Crashlytics. This data does not include your name, location, or any personally identifiable information.

3. Information We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your location.
  • We do not collect your contacts or any data from other apps.
  • We do not require account creation or email registration.
  • We do not store payment information of any kind.
  • We do not use advertising SDKs or track you across other apps.

4. Offline Mode

When you play in offline mode, all game data stays entirely on your device. Nothing is transmitted to any server.

5. Online Multiplayer

Online multiplayer requires a connection to our game server. The following applies to online sessions:

  • Your chosen player name is visible to other players in the same room.
  • Game events are relayed through our server in real time to keep all players synchronised.
  • Sessions are identified by a temporary room code. No account or login is required.
  • All session data is deleted from our server when the game ends or the room is closed.

6. App Permissions

Literature requests the following device permissions:

  • Internet access — required for online multiplayer to connect to the game server and for Firebase Crashlytics to send crash reports. This permission is not used in offline mode beyond crash reporting.
  • Network state access — used to detect when your device loses or regains connectivity so the app can automatically attempt to reconnect during an online game session.

No other device permissions (camera, microphone, contacts, storage, location) are requested or used.

7. Security

All data transmitted between the app and our game server is encrypted in transit using TLS (WSS protocol). Crash reports sent to Firebase Crashlytics are also transmitted over HTTPS. We do not store any personal data on our servers beyond the active game session.

8. Third-Party Services

The app uses the following third-party service:

  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google) — used for automated crash reporting to help us identify and fix bugs. Firebase Crashlytics may collect device information such as device model, OS version, and crash logs. This information does not directly identify you. You can review Google's privacy practices at policies.google.com/privacy.

9. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your information with any third party, except as described in Section 8 (Firebase Crashlytics, solely for crash diagnostics).

10. Data Retention

  • Player name & gameplay data — retained only for the duration of the active game session, then permanently deleted.
  • Crash reports — retained by Firebase Crashlytics for up to 90 days per their standard policy.
  • Onboarding preference — a single flag indicating whether you have completed the onboarding is stored locally on your device. It contains no personal information and can be cleared by uninstalling the app.

11. Children's Privacy

Literature does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. The app does not require any personal information beyond an optional display name chosen by the user.

12. Your Rights

Because we do not maintain user accounts or persistent personal data, there is nothing to access, correct, or delete beyond what is stored locally on your device (which you can clear by uninstalling the app). If you have any questions or concerns, please contact us at the address below.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date at the top. Continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
